ADHD Coaching vs. Therapy

Coaching is present-to-future focused. We build systems, experiments, and habits. Therapy addresses mental health conditions and past healing. We’ll refer out when therapy is the better fit.

About Step Up

Warm, practical, and grounded coaching

I grew up in Russia, where ADHD wasn’t widely understood, and carried my half- recognized challenges into a 30-year career in corporate America. Despite excitement, success, and moments of fulfillment, throughout my career I faced frequent frustration and recurring burnout that I couldn’t fully explain. I recognized these patterns from observing my father’s and grandmother’s behaviors during my childhood, but without a name for them, I didn’t know how to address them and continued to push through the best I could. Everything shifted when my older daughter was diagnosed with ADHD at age 10. That moment launched my journey of self-discovery, helping me recognize that the bright imagination, intuition, and emotional intensity shared by many members of my family were hallmarks of a unique brain style. Through reading, research, and reframing of my own experiences, I learned how mindset and self-compassion can transform struggle into joy. Today, after completing my training with the IACT Center, I am an ADHD Life Coach dedicated to helping others navigate their challenges and find clarity, confidence, and fulfillment.

Philosophy

You’re not broken; your brain is brilliant. We design supports that make your strengths effortless to use. Expect gentle curiosity, clear structure, and actionable tools.

FAQ

Quick answers

Do I need a formal ADHD diagnosis?

Not required. If you’re ADHD-curious or self-identified, coaching can still help. We can also discuss assessment options.

Is coaching covered by insurance?

Coaching is typically not insurance-covered. Many clients use HSA/FSA cards. Receipts provided upon request.

What happens between sessions?

We’ll agree on 1–2 commitments (“step-ups”), plus lightweight check-ins or focus sprints for accountability.
